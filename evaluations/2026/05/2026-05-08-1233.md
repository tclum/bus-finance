**Confidence:** Medium

Conversely, if QCOM and NOW are the new leadership, it implies the AI value chain is moving up-stack toward inference and enterprise applications — which means our own AI tooling investments should prioritize software integration over hardware refresh cycles.

**Confidence:** Medium

Conversely, if energy equities are pricing in a demand slowdown, that is also a leading indicator of broader consumer discretionary weakness, which would pressure our comp-store sales assumptions.

### Recommended investigation

- Retrieve our FY27 fuel-cost budget assumptions and compare to current spot WTI and RBOB futures curves; quantify the P&L impact if spot prices fall 10% from current levels over the next two quarters.

## Hypothesis 4: The Russell 2000's 1.63% decline on elevated volume, combined with financials' underperformance, suggests the market is pricing in a weaker-than-expected jobs report that could delay Fed rate cuts and pressure small-cap credit conditions.

**Category:** Leverage
**Confidence:** Low

The conjunction of small-cap underperformance, financials weakness, and a jobs-report preview suggests the market is pricing in a Goldilocks miss — not weak enough to force the Fed to cut, but weak enough to raise recession concerns. Small caps are more levered than large caps and more sensitive to credit conditions, so a delay in rate cuts would disproportionately pressure RUT.

### Why it matters

If the jobs report tomorrow confirms a slowdown without triggering Fed easing, the cost of capital for small and mid-cap companies rises, which pressures our own supplier base. Many of our private-label and regional suppliers are sub-$1B revenue companies that rely on revolving credit facilities priced off SOFR; if their borrowing costs stay elevated, they will pass those costs through to us via price increases or demand longer payment terms. The CFO needs to model the second-order impact of a "no landing" scenario where growth slows but the Fed holds rates higher for longer.

### Recommended investigation

- Pull the consensus estimate for tomorrow's nonfarm payrolls report and compare to the last three months' prints; if tomorrow's number comes in below 150K (vs. consensus likely around 180K), that confirms the slowdown thesis.
- Compute the spread between the Russell 2000's trailing P/E and the S&P 500's trailing P/E; if the spread has widened over the last month, that confirms small caps are being re-priced for higher cost of capital.
