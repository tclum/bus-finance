**Confidence:** Medium

If our own vendors are exposed to Boeing's supply chain, we could see either (1) pricing relief as suppliers chase volume, or (2) quality/delivery risk if suppliers cut capacity. The CFO needs to assess our aerospace vendor overlap and model the working capital impact if Boeing's order book continues to disappoint.

### Recommended investigation

**Category:** Liquidity
**Confidence:** Low

If the retail sales beat is tax-refund-driven rather than wage-driven, consumer spending will likely decelerate in May–June as the refund tailwind fades and inflation (per the Reuters headline and prior Fed commentary) continues to erode real incomes. This has two implications for the CFO: (1) our own consumer-facing revenue assumptions for Q2 may be too high if we modeled sustained spending growth, and (2) retailers' inventory turnover and days-in-inventory will likely rise in Q2 as sales slow, which could pressure our own inventory if we supply retail channels. The current ratio and quick ratio for consumer-facing names may deteriorate if they overbought inventory assuming sustained demand.

### Recommended investigation
