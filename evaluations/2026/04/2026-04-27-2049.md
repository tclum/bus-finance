## Hypothesis 1: Consumer discretionary and staples margins are facing a 100–200 bps compression risk in Q2 if Brent sustains above $105, with pass-through capacity diverging sharply by pricing power.

**Category:** Profitability
**Confidence:** Medium

### Observation

MCD dropped 3.06% on 1.29× volume and WMT fell 1.79% on 0.91× volume, while XLP declined 1.07% — all underperforming the S&P 500's +0.12%. Reuters headlined "Global consumer companies face pricing stress test from oil shock," and MarketWatch reported Brent crude surpassing $108/barrel with concerns that Iran's limited storage may force production cuts, sustaining elevated prices. The 5-day performance shows MCD down 5.45% and WMT flat at -0.26%, indicating the selloff predates today but accelerated. Notably, consumer discretionary (XLY -0.72%) also lagged, and Domino's Pizza guided weak annual global sales per Reuters, citing consumers curbing spending — a demand-side confirmation that price elasticity is tightening.

### Why it matters

If oil holds above $105, transportation and packaging costs will compress gross margins for any company that cannot immediately reprice. MCD and WMT have historically passed through 60–80% of input cost inflation with a one-quarter lag, but the Domino's guidance suggests consumers are already balking at current price levels. A 10% sustained increase in oil typically translates to 50–100 bps of gross margin pressure for quick-service restaurants and 30–50 bps for big-box retail. If demand elasticity has shifted, the pass-through assumption breaks, and we could see 100–200 bps compression if companies choose volume defense over margin defense.

### Recommended investigation

- Pull MCD, WMT, and DPZ (Domino's) trailing-four-quarter gross margin trends and compute the correlation between Brent crude (lagged one quarter) and gross margin changes to quantify historical pass-through rates.
- Model a sensitivity table: gross margin impact at Brent $105, $110, $115 assuming 50%, 70%, and 90% pass-through rates, and compare to consensus Q2 margin estimates to identify which names have the most downside risk.
- Review MCD and WMT most recent earnings call transcripts for any commentary on oil hedging or forward fuel cost locks; if unhedged, the margin risk is immediate.
- Compare same-store sales growth (SSS) for MCD and DPZ over the last two quarters to gauge whether volume is already weakening, which would confirm the demand-side constraint on pricing power.
