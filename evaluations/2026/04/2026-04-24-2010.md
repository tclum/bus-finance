## Hypothesis 4: Record-low consumer sentiment combined with stable discretionary spending (XLY +0.80%) suggests a divergence between survey-based confidence and actual purchasing behavior that may not persist into Q2.

**Category:** Macro/Strategic
**Confidence:** Low

### Observation

Reuters reported "US consumer sentiment slumps to record low in April; inflation expectations rise," but XLY (Consumer Discretionary) rose +0.80% on 0.59× volume, roughly in line with the S&P 500's +0.80%. Within XLY, AMZN moved +3.49% on 1.14× volume and BKNG +1.99% on 0.82× volume, both discretionary bellwethers. WMT (a Consumer Staples proxy often used as a recession hedge) fell -1.60% on 0.83× volume, and XLP (Staples) fell -0.31% on 0.73× volume. The divergence — record-low sentiment but positive discretionary performance — suggests either the survey is lagging actual behavior, or consumers are spending down savings in a way that is not yet visible in the tape.

### Why it matters

If consumer sentiment is a leading indicator and spending is a lagging indicator, the current divergence implies discretionary spending will weaken in the next 1–2 quarters. For a CFO, this matters because it affects inventory planning and any planned promotional activity — if we assume current spending holds, we risk overordering into a demand cliff. Alternatively, if sentiment surveys have lost predictive power (possible if consumers are desensitized to inflation headlines), then the traditional leading-indicator framework is broken and we need a new early-warning system.

### Recommended investigation

- Pull the University of Michigan Consumer Sentiment Index historical data and overlay it with XLY total return over the trailing 12 months; compute the correlation and assess whether the current divergence is unprecedented or within historical range.
- Check AMZN's most recent quarterly commentary on consumer spending trends (Prime membership growth, AWS revenue as a B2B proxy); if AWS is slowing but retail is stable, it suggests consumer resilience is narrow.
- Pull WMT's most recent same-store sales growth and compare to the prior four quarters; if WMT is underperforming despite being a value retailer, it confirms consumer stress is broadening.
- Model a scenario where discretionary spending falls 5% and 10% in Q2 2026; compute the impact on our own revenue if we have any consumer-facing exposure, and assess whether current inventory levels are appropriate.
